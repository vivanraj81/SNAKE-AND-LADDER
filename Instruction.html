1. Players take turns in clockwise order.
2. On your turn, roll a six-sided die (or spin a spinner with the numbers 1 to 6) to determine how many squares you will move your game piece.
3. Move your game piece forward the number of squares indicated by the die roll. For example, if you roll a 4, move your piece four squares ahead.
4. If your game piece lands on a square with the base of a ladder, you can climb up the ladder, which will take you to the square at the top of the ladder. This is an advantage.
5. If your game piece lands on a square with the head of a snake, you must slide down the snake to the square at its tail. This is a setback.
6. Continue taking turns and moving your game piece until you reach the final square (100) or exceed it.
7. The first player to reach or exceed the final square wins the game.
